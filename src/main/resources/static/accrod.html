Learner's definition of ACCORD
1 
[count] : a formal or official agreement协议；条约
  • The two sides were able to reach an accord.双方得以达成协议。
  • a peace accord和平条约
2 
[noncount] : a situation or state in which people or things agree和谐；一致
  • His ideas and mine were completely/fully in accord. = His ideas were completely/fully in accord with mine. [=his ideas and mine agreed completely]他的想法和我的完全一致。
  • They dressed up for the festival in accord with [=in accordance with] the custom.他们根据节日的习俗盛装打扮。

of its own accord

◊ If something happens of its own accord, it happens by itself without anyone causing it to happen.自发地；自动地
  • The tree fell of its own accord.那棵树自己倒了。

of your own accord

◊ If you do something of your own accord, you do it because you want to, not because someone has asked you or forced you to do it.自愿地；主动地
  • They left of their own accord.他们是自愿离开的。

with one accord

chiefly British, formal一起；同时
: all together
  • They rose with one accord from their seats.他们同时从座位上站了起来。

Learner's definition of ACCORD
[+ object] formal
: to give (something, such as special treatment or status) to someone or something给予（特殊待遇或地位等）；授予
  • He was accorded certain favors because of his age.因年龄关系他受到了一些特殊照顾。
  • Her students accorded her respect. = Her students accorded respect to her. = She was accorded respect by her students.学生们很尊敬她。
  • We accord great importance to education. [=we treat education as very important]我们非常重视教育。

accord with

[phrasal verb]
accord with (something)
: to be in agreement with (something)与…一致
  • His interpretation of the data did not accord with the facts.他对数据的解读与事实不符。
  • His plans for the company did not accord with my own.他对公司的规划和我的不一样。
